Role of colors in Web designing – Case study

September 30, 2017

In today’s era of designing, colors play an imperative role to make your website successful. Colors make the first impression on the visitors’ mind that what is your website about? And what your brand describes?

So, choosing a right palette of colors for websites is an indispensable step to make it more professional, elegant, and successful. Always remember, if you are unable to choose the right colors for your website, you may lose your customers forever and this will create the deterrent impact on your business as well as the position in the market.

Colors spread the emotions and feelings among the people, which nothing else can do. It serves as a means of communication medium. Nowadays, designers take the colors much into consideration to deliver the messages and ideas of their websites.

Colors can influence visitors both psychologically and physiologically. But it depends upon the color scheme that it is going to have a negative or positive impact on the visitors.

Nowadays, multinational companies are investing millions of dollars to decide a right color palette for the packaging and advertising of products. Perfect color schemes not only promote a company, but also help in earning more in business.

Before choosing a color, it is crucial to know the customer’s demands and the needs of the website. One can choose the colors to make a user feel comfortable, relaxed, and secure.

Here is an example, if a website has two colors like black and white than it will definitely look like a newspaper, nothing else. Or if our website is about kids or playground, we cannot make it only in black, white or gray color.

Another example is, if we our website is about any sale and we are using dull colors for our website then surely visitors are not going to believe you and will leave your website forever in just few seconds.

So, it is crucial, especially for the designers to choose the correct color palette to avoid such circumstances which are discussed above. Generally, we have three color groupings: primary, secondary, and tertiary.

* Primary: These colors cannot be formed by mixing any other color.
* Secondary: These colors are the combinations of two primary colors.
* Tertiary: these colors are combinations of one primary and one secondary.

Some practical work

Here is the best example of colors. The consistency of yellow, pink, orange, and purple shows that this can only be applicable for kids. With the help of these colors, the school wants to tell all the parents about the comfortable, fun, warmth, growth, and cheerful environment. Company’s Brochure

The brochure of the company uses the mixture of blue, green, and gray. As these colors have its own specific value. Blue is a Men color. It is known for the professionalism, strength, freedom, and loyalty to the company. The company has a straight message to the people by showing their logo to them. They just believe in success, professionalism, and growth. Moreover, it is also associated with security, trust, and calmness.

Business- Card

The desire of everyone in their life- the business card. The best combination of the colors for graphic designers. A lot of black, shades of gray and some white texture. It gives me the feeling of style, professionalism, creativity, and depth. These types of cards or websites are out of box and presents the elegance and how strong they are.

Red is known for confidence, bravery, emotions, energy, passion, power, but also a danger, warning, heat, fire, stop, anger. In a website, designers use red color for actions like “Book now”, and/or “Click” etcetera.

Orange presents the youth color. It is mostly associated with comfort, fun, enthusiasm, and cheerful atmosphere. It also reflects unsocial, and overly proud. Many technical companies use an orange color based on younger generation concepts.

Yellow shows the creativity, warmth, and optimism. It is associated with sunlight, gold, curiosity, and idealism. But it also creates feelings of anger and illness. Usually, the companies avoid this color for their websites.

Green reflects the progress, peace, health, harmony, money, freshness, and good luck. It is also known for nature, tourism, and helps relieve the stress. It is also a symbol of jealousy and toxicity. It invokes trust and most trendy/ corporate colors.

Blue is a color of strength, reliability, and incredibly high trust value. This color is highly preferred by men. It reflects coolness, freedom, loyalty, but also sadness and depression. It also helps to relax the nervous system. This color is always at the top of High-tech product companies’ list. But we should avoid this as a text color because its not a standard color for the human eye to read.

Violet is known for the royalty, power, wisdom, luxury, magic, and mystery. But it also shows arrogance, immaturity, and ambiguity.

Black is associated with sophistication, strength, depth, but also with death, fear, unhappiness, and evil. It is considered as a stylish and elegant color. Black text color is one of the favorite colors of graphic designers. It is also related to the photography and art.

Gray color of neutrality and indifference. It is associated with reliability, security, but also with sadness, seriousness, conservatism, and loneliness. This color has become a trend for the designers to use it for a text color. As it is more readable.

White is a color of peace, purity, and innocence. Further, it is associated with cleanliness, simplicity, freshness, and clarity. It is also considered for its coldness. It is one of the best background colors of the websites.

Pink is a color of women. It reflects love, romance, softness, sensuality, and innocence. Sometimes it is also known for the weakness, lack of will or power.

Brown is a natural color like for the earth and protection. It is associated reliability, security, comfort, confidence, but also sometimes for heaviness and boredom. Usually, the websites of coffee prefers this color.

Conclusion

If you are going to choose colors for your website or for any art related activities. Be sure to choose “fewer colors, but enough”. Your page should have fewer pallete, but with this one can use some shades, tones or tints.

Avoid overdo. Colors should not confuse the visitors or customers. For instance, if you are making a website for man. Always use black, blue, Gray color which represents the passion, bold, enthusiastic. We cannot use the color pink or yellow color for the business concept.

Always try to understand the customer’s needs. What he wants from a website? What the website is about? Who is the customer of his business? Make the life of your customer easier so that he never leaves you.
